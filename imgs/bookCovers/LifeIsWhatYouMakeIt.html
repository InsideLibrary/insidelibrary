Book Review: Life Is What You Make It

Title:

Life Is What You Make It

Author:

Preeti Shenoy

Publication:

Westland Publications

Publication Year:

2011

Genre:

Fiction, Inspirational

Language:

English (Original)

Number of Pages:

288

About the Author

Preeti Shenoy is a bestselling Indian author known for her captivating storytelling and deep understanding of human emotions. With a background in commerce and an MBA, she has authored several novels that explore themes of love, relationships, and personal growth. Shenoy's works often resonate with readers, making her one of India's most popular contemporary writers. In addition to her novels, she is a motivational speaker and an advocate for mental health awareness.

About the Book

*Life Is What You Make It* tells the story of a young woman named Ankita, who navigates the challenges of life, love, and mental health. The novel explores her journey through the complexities of relationships and the struggle to find her identity amidst societal pressures. Preeti Shenoy addresses significant issues like mental health, ambition, and the pursuit of happiness, ultimately conveying that life is shaped by our choices and perspectives. The narrative is both engaging and thought-provoking, encouraging readers to reflect on their own lives.

Language of the Book and Writing Style

Shenoy’s writing is smooth, engaging, and emotionally resonant. Her use of relatable language and vivid descriptions brings the characters and their experiences to life. The book is structured in a way that balances storytelling with deeper messages, making it both an enjoyable and insightful read. Shenoy’s compassionate and empathetic tone fosters a connection between the reader and the characters, encouraging reflection on personal choices and the impact of mental health.

My Opinion About the Book

I found *Life Is What You Make It* to be a compelling and impactful read. Preeti Shenoy's ability to tackle complex themes like mental health and self-discovery with sensitivity and realism is commendable. The story serves as a powerful reminder that our lives are defined by the choices we make and the attitudes we adopt. While the themes may resonate with many readers, Shenoy's unique narrative style and heartfelt approach make this book a valuable addition to contemporary literature. It is an excellent choice for anyone seeking inspiration and insight into navigating life's challenges.
