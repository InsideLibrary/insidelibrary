About the Author

"The Psychology of Money" this marvallous book us penned down by one of the best writers of all time "Morgan Housel." He is the New York Times Best Selling author of "The Psychology of Money" and "Same As Ever." His books have sold over six million copies and have been translated into more than 50 languages across the globe. He is two time winner of "The Best in Business" award from the society of American Business Editors and Writers and winner of the New York times Sidney Award. About the Book

We always keep thinking that, even if people have lot of money, they live a mediocre life & some people with less moneys in long term they become millionaire. In this miraculous book writer reveals the secrete behind this all difference. This book examines personal finance through the lens of human behavior, It's a fresh take on a well-trod subject; Housel's focus is the relationship between people & money from this book readers krow that to grasp why people bury themselves in debt you don't need to smart study intrest rates, you need to study histoty of greed, insecurity and optimism. Doing well with money has very little to do with how smart you are and a lot to do with how you behave. Each of the chapters in the book explores an individual human behavior or attitude towards money. Certain behaviour induce positive outcomes while others guarantee failure. This book is divided into multiple chapters, some lessons caution us against certain behaviours, other lessons encourage us to embrace benifical habits. The beauty and speciality of this book is lessons in this book are accessible to everyone, they are not limited for some special or rich highly qualified people.

Language of the Book and Writing Style

Author have used very Language of simples clear & straight forword language while composing this ground breaking book. He also have used some wisdoms and proverb to impact easy understanding to readers & also to give deep knowladge in minimum content. This book has total 256 pages divided into 19 chapters, delves into the various facets of money management.
