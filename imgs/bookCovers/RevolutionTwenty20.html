Book Review: Revolution 2020

Title:

Revolution 2020: Love, Corruption, Ambition

Author:

Chetan Bhagat

Publication:

Rupa Publications

Publication Year:

2011

Genre:

Contemporary Fiction, Drama

Language:

English (Original)

About the Author

Chetan Bhagat is a renowned Indian author, known for his novels that capture the dreams, aspirations, and struggles of India’s youth. With a background in engineering from IIT and an MBA from IIM, Bhagat's books often reflect the social and educational pressures in modern India. His simple writing style and relatable characters have made him a household name, especially among young readers. "Revolution 2020" is one of his works that combines themes of love, politics, and corruption, and it reflects his ability to blend storytelling with social commentary.

About the Book

"Revolution 2020" follows the lives of three childhood friends: Gopal, Raghav, and Aarti. Set in Varanasi, the novel delves into the ambitions and struggles of these characters as they navigate love, success, and the corrupt education system. Gopal, the protagonist, comes from a poor family and dreams of becoming successful by any means necessary, even if it involves unethical practices. Raghav, his friend, chooses the path of honesty and idealism, determined to fight corruption through journalism. Aarti, the love interest, is torn between the two. The novel addresses the intersection of personal ambition and the larger political and social realities in India, focusing on the choices young people have to make in a world dominated by greed and corruption.

Language of the Book and Writing Style

Chetan Bhagat’s writing in "Revolution 2020" is direct, simple, and conversational, making it an easy read for a wide audience. He uses everyday language to tell the story, which resonates with young readers, especially those from small towns who can relate to the struggles of the protagonists. Bhagat’s writing is filled with emotional intensity, and he does well in creating a balance between romance, ambition, and social issues. The pacing of the book is fast, with enough twists and emotional moments to keep readers engaged across its 296 pages.

My Opinion About the Book

"Revolution 2020" is a thought-provoking novel that reflects the challenges of modern India, particularly its corrupt educational system and the moral dilemmas faced by the youth. Gopal’s character, with his moral compromises, feels real and relatable, and the love triangle between Gopal, Raghav, and Aarti adds emotional depth to the story. The novel successfully brings to light the difficult choices people make in the pursuit of success, and how these choices impact relationships and society. While some may find the storyline predictable or melodramatic at times, Bhagat’s ability to blend romance with social commentary makes "Revolution 2020" an engaging read for anyone interested in contemporary Indian fiction.
