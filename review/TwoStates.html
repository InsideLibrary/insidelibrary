Book Review: 2 States: The Story of My Marriage

Title:

2 States: The Story of My Marriage

Author:

Chetan Bhagat

Publication:

Rupa Publications

Publication Year:

2009

Genre:

Contemporary Romance, Drama

Language:

English (Original)

About the Author

Chetan Bhagat, known for his relatable, light-hearted storytelling, is one of India’s most widely read authors. With a background in engineering from IIT and an MBA from IIM, his novels often reflect the aspirations and challenges of young Indians navigating love, career, and family expectations. "2 States: The Story of My Marriage" is based on Bhagat’s own experiences and portrays the cultural clashes faced by a couple coming from different parts of India.

About the Book

"2 States: The Story of My Marriage" is a humorous yet heartwarming tale about Krish, a Punjabi boy from Delhi, and Ananya, a Tamil Brahmin girl from Chennai, who fall in love while studying at IIM Ahmedabad. As they decide to marry, the real challenge begins—convincing their culturally diverse families. The novel takes the reader on a journey through the ups and downs of cross-cultural relationships, shedding light on the stark differences between North and South Indian traditions. Bhagat weaves a narrative filled with love, humor, and the complex dynamics of family, highlighting how love can bridge even the widest cultural gaps.

Language of the Book and Writing Style

Chetan Bhagat’s writing in "2 States" is simple, witty, and conversational, making it an easy and enjoyable read. He uses humor and sarcasm to address serious themes like cultural differences, parental pressure, and the complexities of marriage in India. The characters of Krish and Ananya are well-crafted, and their banter adds to the charm of the book. Bhagat’s storytelling is light-hearted but also manages to capture the emotional struggles faced by the couple. The novel is fast-paced and keeps readers engaged with its mix of romance, comedy, and family drama across 269 pages.

My Opinion About the Book

"2 States: The Story of My Marriage" is a fun and entertaining read that explores the cultural diversity of India through the lens of a modern love story. Bhagat’s ability to address the tensions and humor in inter-caste and inter-state marriages makes the book both relevant and relatable. The story is filled with moments of laughter, family drama, and emotional depth as Krish and Ananya strive to get their families on board with their relationship. While the plot may be predictable at times, the book’s charm lies in its simplicity and the relatable struggles of young love in a traditional society. It’s a light, feel-good read perfect for anyone who enjoys a contemporary romance with a strong cultural backdrop.
