Book Review: Hamlet

Title:

Hamlet

Author:

Shakespeare

Publication:

Nicolas Ling and John Trundell

Publication Year:

1603

Genre:

Imaginary

Language:

English (Original Language)

About the Author

This ground breaking play was written by very famous dramatist and play writter "William Shakespeare." His work still existence, including some collaborations, consists of thiry-eight plays, one hundread and fifty four sonnets, two long narrative poems and several other playwrights and have been translated in allmost all major languages. "Romeo Juliet" is his most famous romantic tragedy of sexually charged adolescence, love and death. His plays demonstrate the expanssiveness of his imagination and the extent of his learning. A true genius, Shakespeare's popular character and plots are studied, performed, reinterpretend and discussed till today. William Shakespeare, known as the English national poet, is considered as the greatest dramatist of all time.

About the Book

A typical Elizabethon revenge play Hamlet is Shakespeare's longest play and one of the most quoted works in English language. It is described as "the world most filmed story after Cindrella" The main theme of this book is "play inside a play" or "drama inside a drama." It can be consider as one of the most groundbteaking and magical drama book because though it was written in early sevententh century and till it has the power and that magic to make audence (readeres) fall in love with it. It is one of the most widely translated books in the world. As it is the written form of play, it includes lots of drama, imaginary miracles, trajedy, romance, love and a story of betrayal. Speciality of this book is it includes lots of plots as per the sequence of the story and the reason behind the great success of this book is the imagination of Sir William Shakespeare.

Language of the Book and Writing Style

Writer have used very sweet and beautiful language in this book . As it a written form of play it also includes some poetry. It is a medium size book with 200 pages. It includes total 5 act and 20 scences. We also find the authentic English in this book.
